Mansion of Time

"Mansion of Time" is a 2.5D point-and-click puzzle adventure game.
In this game you are trapped in a time loop on the 1920s on Halloween.
To return into your own time, you have to solve the puzzles and mysteries in the mansion and repair your time machine.

  • Duration:
  • Release Date:
  • Purpose:
  • Links:
  • ~6 months
  • April 2024
  • Uni Project 3rd Semester
  • .exe itch.io PPX

    • Process

    We worked in a group of six. We had 4 artists, two 2D artists, one 3D artist and our art lead, who pretty much did anything else that was needed as well as the sound. The other two of us were programmer.
    I also was the producer of the team and programming wise I did the entire UI and anything that came with it. I designed most of it, programmed the inventory, dialogue system, the settings, the hints and trigger boxes and the saving system of the game.
    In the very end then I pretty much worked within every other script as well to fix the bugs and to polish the game - I worked with the character movement, the interactable system, camera, the spawning, traps and puzzles.
    It was our vision to create a game like the movies "Groundhog Day" and "Edge of Tomorrow", where the player has to learn step by step, what the right way is, pay attention to details and puzzle the solution together step by step.

    What I learned

    Of course, I learned quite a lot about the programs we worked with – unity, codecks, miro, perforce and certainly about C# and programming.
    But the thing I think I learned the most about is being a producer and to lead a working group.
    I learned that you can not really depend on, that others will manage their time themselves and should have talked about that with everyone way earlier in detail.
    I also learned that discussing everything with everyone is not the right way. Of course, everyone’s ideas are amazing, and everyone should see a part of their own vision within the game but if you are in a big group, it takes a lot of time when you only decide on something, when everyone is happy with something.
    Also, I realized that if you do not ask people on some matters when you simply need a decision soon, that mostly no one minds and is actually happy, that they don’t have to care.
    I learned, that it’s best to rather present an idea which then can be adjusted together in the group, than to present open questions.

    Hopsie Flopsie

    Hopsy Flopsy is a touch-based 3D Jump'n'Run Action-Adventure game in angled-top-down perspective with a humorous aspect.
    The game is made for mobile devices. Play a sea creature trying to cross the arduos city to reach the ocean!

  • Duration:
  • Release Date:
  • Purpose:
  • Links:
  • ~6 months
  • September 2023
  • Uni Project 2nd Semester
  • Git .exe .apk itch.io GDD

    • Process

    In a group of ten we worked together on “Hopsy Flopsy”. We were 5 artists, of which I worked closest with the UI artist and art lead. We had one producer, who also programmed, and 4 programmers, of which one took over the sound.
    My task was the UI, and anything connected, meaning the design of the UI, the programming of it, anything there was to save in the game, the winning condition and rating of the level, the timer, the death, and the collectables.
    In the beginning I worked with Unity's old UI system, but I really wished for uniform buttons, which you could all change with one click. Because of that, someone suggested to me to look into Unity's new UI toolki. In the end, I really liked the toolkit, but it was very hard for me to work with it especially in the beginning, because since the toolkit was so new there were very very few tutorials to help me.
    And I drew the little sea creature icons for each of our team memerbs in the credits.

    What I learned

    I learned that a game is a looooooot of work.
    In the beginning I thought that we would surely be able to do way more. More levels, more features, more skins, more everything.
    But in the beginning, I did not consider all the little details, which also need so much work, the bugs I got stuck in and which consumed unexpected much time, and just anything that you can not think of in the beginning and which you learn about on the way.
    And, of course, I learned a lot about programming, the engine Unity, and especially about UI.
    The UI design, which I am now also paying way much more attention to in other games, the logic behind it, the animations on hover and on click and anything else that is moving via sprites, like the waterbar.
    And I learned that the more communication the better, especially if you have to work with people from other departments.

    • Process

    My mother always asked me, as I am a programmer, if I couldn't 'just' also program the website for her Bed 'n Breakfast.
    When then in our Graphics Design class we were allowed to choose any topic, we covered for the homework for our exam and as we also had a lecture about HTML, I decided to program this website which I could also give my mother for her website, so she does not have to pay her current website host that much anymore each month.
    Unfortunately, the contract with her current website host always goes for 2 years and as my professor said it was enough to have one web page, there only is the home page done for now.
    But in the future, I definitely plan to end this, so that my mother can use this one instead!
    The website I did is very similar to the one which is online for our Pension right now (pension-schultze.de), as my mother is very happy with the current design, and after I did research of many other hotel websites I agreed and I also like this design, as most are very full and it is sometimes quite confusing to get the pieces of information you actually want.
    Still, I didn't copy one line of code from the original and also did some few adjustments.

    Carrot Cutter

    We built a robot that cuts fruits and vegetables into even pieces. He can recognize the size of objects, divide them into equal-sized pieces and cut them accordingly. Maybe he should also push the pieces into a basket. The size is recognized by a camera. Since the motors we have available are not particularly powerful, our biggest focus is on finding a design that can generate enough power to cut even harder vegetables like carrots.
