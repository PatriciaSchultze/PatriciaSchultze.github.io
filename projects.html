Mansion of Time

"Mansion of Time" is a 2.5D point-and-click puzzle adventure game.
In this game you are trapped in a time loop in the 1920s on Halloween.
To return into your own time, you have to solve the puzzles and mysteries within the mansion and repair your time machine.

  • Release Date:
  • Purpose:
  • Links:

  • April 2024
  • Uni Project 3rd Semester

    • Process

    We worked in a group of six. We were 2 programmers and 4 artists.
    We had two 2D artists, one 3D artist and our art lead, who pretty much did anything else that was needed as well as the sound. The other two of us were programmers.
    I was the producer of the team and next to organizing, talking to everyone, and planning, I also programmed. I did the entire UI and anything that came with it - I designed most of it, programmed the inventory and items, the dialogue system, the settings, the hints and trigger boxes and the saving system of the game.
    In the very end then I pretty much worked within every other script as well to fix the bugs and to polish the game - I worked with the character movement, the interactable system, camera, the spawning, traps and puzzles and more.
    It was our vision to create a game like the movies "Groundhog Day" and "Edge of Tomorrow", where the player has to learn step by step, what the right way is, pay attention to details and puzzle the solution together step by step.

    What I learned

    Of course, I learned quite a lot about the programs we worked with – unity, codecks, miro, perforce and certainly about C# and programming.
    But the thing I think I learned most about is being a producer and how to lead a working group.
    In the beginning I thought as a producer it would be my main task to figure out, what needs to be done and talk with everyone who does what until what point. But only in the process of working I realized, I should have talked to everyone about their tasks in a way more detailed way and make more precise plans for each weeks’ goals instead just having this big list for everyone for only the milestones. I started this way too late in this project and I will definitely try to do better next time.
    I also learned that discussing everything with everyone is not the right way. Of course, everyone’s ideas are amazing, and everyone should see a part of their own vision within the game but if you are in a big group, it takes a lot of time when you only decide on something, when everyone is happy with something.
    So, I realized, that if you do not ask people on some matters when you simply need a decision soon, that mostly no one minds and is actually happy, that they don’t have to care.
    I learned, that it’s best to rather present an idea which then can be adjusted together in the group, than to present open questions.

    In a group of ten we worked together on “Hopsy Flopsy”.
    We were 5 artists, of which I worked closest with the UI artist and art lead. We had one producer, who also programmed, and 4 programmers, of which one took over the sound.

    Carrot Cutter

    We built a robot that cuts fruits and vegetables into even pieces. He can recognize the size of objects, divide them into equal-sized pieces and cut them accordingly.
    The size is recognized by a camera. Since the motors we had available are not particularly powerful, our biggest focus was on finding a design that can generate enough power to cut even harder vegetables like carrots.

  • Duration:
  • Release Date:
  • Purpose:
  • Links:
  • ~6 months
  • July 2022
  • uni - TU lab
  • docu PPX code.zip

    • Process

    In a group of 4 we built a robot to cut fruit and vegetables. The idea to build such robot came, as I am nearly daily cutting carrots for my horse and what a great idea to have a machine doing that for you!
    So that, on the one hand, the carrot pieces ultimately have the same volume, no matter how wide the carrot itself is, and, on the other hand, the robot receives the information about where to start and where to stop cutting, we needed image processing. Programming this was my task.
    To do this, we installed a camera whose colored image is converted into a black and white image using an existing program. We were able to process the camera image in Processing, but not in the Arduino program. Therefore, we wrote everything that has to do with the image in Processing and then transferred the data. Due to this simplification, the board that the carrot is on is white and the carrot is black on the screen.

    Fire and Water

    This is a Co-op 2D jump and run game.
    There are 2 characters, one fire char (red) and one water char (blue), which each are dying, if they touch the ground of the other element. All items (the squares) have to be collected to open the end doors.

  • Duration:
  • Release Date:
  • Purpose:
  • Links:
  • ~1,5 days
  • March 2024
  • Software Engineering Exam
  • Git docu

    • Process

    This game is very much inspired by the game 'Fireboy & Watergirl'.
    This exam was about to write as clean code as possible and think and plan beforehand, how we want and have to connect each script. For this we were supposed to choose a simple game.
    Since I have loved to play the 'Fireboy & Watergirl' game, I choose to program this. As in the two big projects, Mansion of Time and Hopsy Flopsy, I did before, I was mainly responsible for the UI and saving everything and then in the end bug fixing everywhere, I knew my way around the engine, but I actually never did character movement or any physics from scratch.
    It was very fun to me, doing something different here!

    What I learned

    I was very surprised, how fast this game was to program and was very excited about that, especially, when I watched other people play this game.
    It was so interesting to see the different strategies, skills and approaches. Some people needed only around 10 minutes, others even an whole hour, but none of them gave up and I could see their fun and progress.

    On the one hand this showed me again, why I love t to be a game designer. And on the other hand this gave me the idea for our next group project: Duo Dash. If I was able to do a fun game within 1,5 days alone, what could we do in a group of 8 together within 6 months?

    First Unreal Game

    A small jump and run, where you have to collect 5 coins, each connected to a little challenge.

  • Duration:
  • Release Date:
  • Purpose:
  • Links:
  • ~2 days
  • December 2022
  • Uni Project
  • .exe

    • Process

    The requirements for this were to aim for ~5min playtime and having a puzzle in a little jump an run.
    I decided, that the player shall collect 5 coins, which I each connect with one challenge.
    As back then my programming skills were quite at their beginning, I had to cancel two originally planned challenges. Instead I made it quite easy to reach two out of the five coins, by simply putting them on the ground. But I did not just place the coins randomly, but wanted to encourage th player to walk around the whole map and by that find all clues for the first riddle.
    The first riddle is a color-form puzzle, for which you have to find and see all objects with a special material and with that combine the code for a door. The other two challenges were jump and run challenges for which you had to overcome disappearing and also moving platforms.
